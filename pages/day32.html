Target Wake Time (TWT)
for Power Saving

A battery IoT sensor that reports once a minute shouldn't burn power listening for 59 seconds of nothing. Target Wake Time lets a client and AP agree on exactly when the client will wake, so it can sleep deeply the rest of the time. Today: individual and broadcast TWT.

The Power Problem

Legacy power save (PS-Poll, then 802.11n's U-APSD) still requires the client to wake at every beacon (~every 100 ms) to check the TIM. For a coin-cell sensor, that adds up. TWT changes the model from "wake often and check" to "sleep until an agreed time."

TWT can extend IoT battery life dramatically.
By negotiating a wake schedule measured in seconds or minutes instead of milliseconds, a device's radio can stay off almost all the time. Vendors report multi-x battery improvements for sensors and wearables — one of Wi-Fi 6's most underappreciated features.
⏰

Individual TWT

In individual TWT, a single client negotiates its own schedule with the AP via TWT Setup action frames. The agreement specifies:

  • TWT Wake Time: the target time of the first wake (referenced to the AP's TSF).
  • TWT Wake Interval: how often the wake recurs.
  • TWT Wake Duration: how long the client stays awake each time (the Service Period).
  • Trigger-enabled: whether the AP will send a Trigger frame during the SP (for UL OFDMA) or the client just transmits.
  • Announced vs Unannounced: whether the client sends a PS-Poll/QoS-Null to signal it's awake, or the AP transmits immediately at the scheduled time.
📢

Broadcast TWT

Negotiating per-client schedules doesn't scale to hundreds of devices. Broadcast TWT lets the AP advertise a shared schedule in beacons; clients simply join a TWT group. This is ideal for large fleets of similar IoT devices and also underpins features like staggering wake times to reduce contention bursts.

Setup:  STA <-> AP negotiate wake_time, interval=60s, duration=10ms

🧠 Knowledge Check
What does TWT negotiate?

Scheduled wake times and intervals so a device can sleep in between.

Individual vs broadcast TWT?

Individual is a per-client agreement; broadcast is a shared schedule advertised in beacons.

What is TWT's main benefit?

Large battery savings for IoT via a very low duty cycle.
