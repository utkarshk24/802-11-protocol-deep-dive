Lesson 19 Phase 3 · 802.11ac (WiFi 5)

Why 802.11ac
Was Revolutionary

802.11ac (Wi-Fi 5, 2013) was the jump to gigabit Wi-Fi. It did it by going all-in on 5 GHz, doubling and quadrupling channel width, adding 256-QAM, up to 8 spatial streams, and multi-user beamforming. Today we set the stage for the whole VHT phase.

The Four Big Levers

802.11ac (Very High Throughput, VHT) pushed data rates from 802.11n's ~600 Mbps to a theoretical ~6.9 Gbps by turning four dials at once:

  • Wider channels: 20 → 40 → 80 → 160 MHz. Double the bandwidth, double the rate.
  • Denser modulation: Added 256-QAM (8 bits/symbol) on top of 64-QAM — a 33% raw gain when SNR allows.
  • More spatial streams: Up to 8 streams via MIMO (though most clients do 1–2).
  • MU-MIMO (downlink): The AP transmits to multiple clients simultaneously using beamforming, instead of time-slicing.
802.11ac is 5 GHz only.
VHT operates exclusively in the 5 GHz band, where wide contiguous channels actually exist. 2.4 GHz stays on 802.11n (HT). This is why dual-band APs advertise 802.11n on 2.4 GHz and 802.11ac on 5 GHz simultaneously.
📊

Data Rate Progression

The peak PHY rate is the product of bandwidth, modulation density, coding, and streams. A few reference points (long GI, single stream unless noted):

text VHT Rate Milestones 
Config                                  Peak PHY rate
20 MHz, 64-QAM,  1 SS  (802.11n-like)     ~65 Mbps
80 MHz, 256-QAM, 1 SS                     ~433 Mbps
80 MHz, 256-QAM, 2 SS                     ~867 Mbps
160 MHz, 256-QAM, 2 SS                    ~1.73 Gbps
160 MHz, 256-QAM, 8 SS (theoretical max) ~6.93 Gbps

Real-world throughput is roughly half the PHY rate after MAC overhead, contention, and retries — but even so, 802.11ac made 1 Gbps Wi-Fi a reality for the first time.

802.11n vs 802.11ac at a Glance

text HT vs VHT 
Feature            802.11n (HT)      802.11ac (VHT)
Bands              2.4 + 5 GHz       5 GHz only
Max channel width  40 MHz            160 MHz
Max modulation     64-QAM            256-QAM
Max spatial streams 4                8
MU-MIMO            no                yes (downlink)
Beamforming        optional/messy    standardized (sounding)
Peak PHY rate      600 Mbps          6.93 Gbps

Which band does 802.11ac operate in?
