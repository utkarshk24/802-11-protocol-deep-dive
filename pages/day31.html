MU-OFDMA &
SU-OFDMA Modes

OFDMA and MU-MIMO are often confused because both serve multiple users. They're orthogonal techniques — one splits the channel in frequency, the other in space — and 802.11ax can combine them. Today we separate the modes cleanly and see when each wins.

Two Orthogonal Axes

text Frequency vs Space 
OFDMA   = split the channel in FREQUENCY (different RUs)
MU-MIMO = split the channel in SPACE     (different spatial streams,
                                          same frequency, via beamforming)

They are independent. You can do:
  - OFDMA only            (users on different RUs)
  - MU-MIMO only          (users on same RU, separated by beams)
  - OFDMA + MU-MIMO       (multiple users per RU, multiple RUs)
Rule of thumb: OFDMA for small packets, MU-MIMO for big ones.
OFDMA shines when many clients have small payloads (IoT, chat, ACKs) — packing them into RUs kills overhead. MU-MIMO shines when a few clients have large payloads and good SNR — spatial streams multiply throughput. 802.11ax schedulers mix both.
📖

SU-OFDMA vs MU-OFDMA

  • SU-OFDMA: A single client is assigned one or more RUs in an otherwise multi-user PPDU. It still benefits from the finer RU structure (e.g. avoiding a narrowband interferer by using a clean RU), but only one user is served.
  • MU-OFDMA: Multiple clients each get their own RU in the same PPDU — the true multi-user case that delivers the density gains.
⬇️

Downlink vs Uplink OFDMA

Downlink (DL) OFDMA is straightforward: the AP owns the medium and simply transmits different RUs to different clients in one PPDU. It signals the allocation in HE-SIG-B and everyone decodes their slice.

Uplink (UL) OFDMA is harder and brand-new in 802.11ax: multiple clients must transmit at the same time on their assigned RUs, arriving at the AP aligned in time, frequency, and power. This is impossible with random access, so the AP uses a Trigger frame to command exactly who transmits where — covered on Day 33.

text DL vs UL OFDMA 
DL-OFDMA:  AP ----[RU1:A][RU2:B][RU3:C]----> A, B, C   (one PPDU)

UL-OFDMA:  AP --[Trigger: A->RU1, B->RU2, C->RU3]-->
           then A, B, C all transmit at once on their RUs -> AP
           AP --[Multi-STA Block Ack]--> acknowledges all three
