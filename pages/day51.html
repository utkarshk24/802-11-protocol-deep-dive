Regulatory, DFS & TPC
802.11h & 6 GHz AFC

Wi-Fi shares spectrum with radar, satellites, and licensed incumbents. Which channels you may use, and at what power, is dictated by regulators — and the protocol has machinery (Country IE, DFS, TPC, AFC) to enforce coexistence. Skip this and you'll misdiagnose half of all "why won't it use that channel?" problems.

Regulatory Domains & the Country IE

Every region (FCC/US, ETSI/EU, MIC/Japan, …) defines which channels are legal and the maximum power for each. An AP advertises its regulatory domain in the Country Information Element in every beacon, so clients automatically obey local rules:

text Country IE (Element ID 7) 
Country String : "US" + environment byte
Triplets (repeated):
  First Channel = 36
  Number of Channels = 4        -> channels 36,40,44,48
  Max TX Power = 23 dBm

  First Channel = 149
  Number of Channels = 4        -> channels 149,153,157,161
  Max TX Power = 30 dBm

A client that hears this restricts itself accordingly. Devices without
a country hint often fall back to a conservative "world" domain.
This is why the same AP shows different channels abroad.
Channels 12–13 (2.4 GHz) are legal in Europe but not the US; many 5 GHz channels differ too. The Country IE + the device's own regulatory database intersect to decide the usable set. A mismatch (e.g. a US client near an EU AP) is a common "can't see the network" cause.

802.11h: DFS & TPC for 5 GHz Coexistence

Large parts of the 5 GHz band (the U-NII-2 range) are shared with weather and military radar. 802.11h adds two mechanisms so Wi-Fi doesn't interfere:

DFS — Dynamic Frequency Selection

  • Channel Availability Check (CAC): before using a DFS channel, the AP must listen for radar for a period (typically 60 s; up to 600 s on weather-radar channels). During CAC it can't transmit — which is why DFS APs take a minute to come up.
  • In-service monitoring: the AP keeps watching for radar. On detection it must vacate within seconds, announcing the move with a Channel Switch Announcement (Lesson 15) so clients follow instead of dropping.
  • Non-Occupancy Period: a channel where radar was seen is off-limits for ~30 minutes.

TPC — Transmit Power Control

Stations advertise their power capability and can be told to reduce transmit power to the minimum needed for a good link, limiting interference to incumbents and neighbors. The Power Constraint and TPC Report elements (Lesson 9) carry this.

DFS is the reason 160 MHz is hard (Lesson 24).
Contiguous 160 MHz channels almost always include DFS sub-channels, inheriting the CAC delay and radar-eviction risk. Many client devices historically avoided DFS entirely — which is why "just enable 160 MHz" so often disappoints in practice.
🛰️

6 GHz: Power Classes & AFC

The 6 GHz band (Wi-Fi 6E/7) is shared with licensed fixed-link and satellite incumbents, so it introduced a new coexistence model based on power classes:

ClassWherePowerControl
LPI (Low Power Indoor)indoor onlylowno coordination; no external antenna
VLP (Very Low Power)indoor/outdoorvery lowportable devices, no coordination
SP (Standard Power)indoor/outdoorhighrequires AFC

AFC (Automated Frequency Coordination) is a cloud database an SP access point must query with its geolocation. The AFC returns which 6 GHz channels and power levels are safe at that location (protecting nearby incumbent fixed links). Only after that clearance can an SP AP use high power outdoors — a fundamentally new, database-driven regulatory model for Wi-Fi.

Compute It: Parse the Country IE

python country_ie.py 
from scapy.all import rdpcap, Dot11Beacon, Dot11Elt

def parse_country(info):
    country = info[:2].decode(errors="ignore")
    body = info[3:]                       # skip 2-char country + env byte
    triplets = []
    for i in range(0, len(body) - 2, 3):
        first_ch, n_ch, max_pwr = body[i], body[i+1], body[i+2]
        triplets.append((first_ch, n_ch, max_pwr))
    return country, triplets

for pkt in rdpcap("capture.pcap"):
    if pkt.haslayer(Dot11Beacon):
        elt = pkt.getlayer(Dot11Elt)
        while elt:
            if elt.ID == 7:                    # Country element
                country, triplets = parse_country(bytes(elt.info))
                print(f"Regulatory domain: {country}")
                for first, n, pwr in triplets:
                    chans = [first + 4*k for k in range(n)]
                    print(f"  channels {chans}  max {pwr} dBm")
                break
            elt = elt.payload.getlayer(Dot11Elt)
        break
