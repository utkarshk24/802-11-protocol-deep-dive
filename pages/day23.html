Spatial Streams & MIMO

Spatial multiplexing sends independent data streams over multiple antennas at the same time and frequency. The receiver, using multiple antennas and channel estimates from the VHT-LTF fields, mathematically separates them. N streams ≈ N× the data rate — as long as the channel is "rich" (lots of multipath) and SNR is high.

text MIMO Notation 
"4x4:3"  =  4 TX antennas  ×  4 RX antennas  :  3 spatial streams

Antennas >= streams (extra antennas improve SNR / diversity).
Effective streams = min(TX streams, RX streams, channel rank).
More antennas than streams is deliberate.
A 4x4:3 radio uses its 4th antenna for diversity and beamforming gain, not a 4th stream. Extra receive chains raise SNR, which lets the link hold a higher MCS — sometimes worth more than another stream.
🛡️

STBC — Trading Streams for Range

Space-Time Block Coding does the opposite of spatial multiplexing: it sends the same data across multiple antennas with a clever time/space encoding. You don't gain speed — you gain diversity, so the signal survives fading and reaches farther. STBC is a range/reliability tool for cell-edge clients, signaled by 1 bit in VHT-SIG-A.

🧬

LDPC vs BCC Coding

802.11ac supports two forward-error-correction codes:

  • BCC (Binary Convolutional Coding): Mandatory, simpler, lower latency, less silicon.
  • LDPC (Low-Density Parity-Check): Optional, ~1.5–2 dB coding gain — meaning it decodes correctly at lower SNR. That gain translates to either more range at a given rate or a higher rate at a given range.

Both AP and client must support LDPC (advertised in the VHT Capabilities IE) to use it. When they do, marginal links get noticeably more robust for essentially free.

text Reliability Toolbox Summary 
Technique       Gives you            Costs you
Spatial mux     more throughput      needs high SNR + rich channel
STBC            more range/diversity no throughput gain
LDPC            ~1.5-2 dB SNR margin  more decoder complexity
Beamforming     focused SNR gain     sounding overhead (Day 25)
