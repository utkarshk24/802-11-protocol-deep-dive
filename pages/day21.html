VHT Capabilities
& Negotiation

Two 802.11ac devices rarely have identical abilities. The VHT Capabilities Information Element, exchanged in beacons, probes, and association frames, lets them agree on the fastest common configuration. Today we decode it bit by bit.

The VHT Capabilities IE (ID 191)

This IE has two parts: a 4-byte VHT Capabilities Info field and an 8-byte Supported VHT-MCS and NSS Set. Key fields in the info word:

  • Max MPDU Length: 3895 / 7991 / 11454 bytes — bigger frames = less overhead.
  • Supported Channel Width Set: whether the device does 160 MHz and/or 80+80 MHz.
  • Rx/Tx LDPC: support for the stronger low-density parity-check coding.
  • Short GI for 80/160 MHz: 400 ns guard interval support.
  • SU/MU Beamformer & Beamformee: who can steer beams to whom.
  • Max A-MPDU Length Exponent: ceiling on aggregation.
🧮

The MCS & NSS Map

The Supported VHT-MCS Set uses 2 bits per spatial stream to advertise the highest MCS that stream supports:

text Per-SS MCS Encoding 
2-bit value   Meaning
  0           MCS 0-7  supported
  1           MCS 0-8  supported (adds 256-QAM 3/4)
  2           MCS 0-9  supported (adds 256-QAM 5/6)
  3           stream NOT supported

Example: a 2x2 client advertises SS1=2, SS2=2, SS3-8=3
  => "MCS 0-9 on two streams, no streams 3-8"
Negotiation = intersection.
The effective configuration is the minimum of both sides: the narrowest common channel width, the fewest common streams, and the lowest common max MCS. A 4x4 AP talking to a 1x1 phone runs at 1 stream. This is why a fast AP doesn't rescue a cheap client.

Decoding the Capabilities IE

python vht_caps.py 
import struct
from scapy.all import rdpcap, Dot11Elt

def parse_vht_caps(info):
    cap = struct.unpack("<I", info[:4])[0]
    width = (cap >> 2) & 0x3
    width_txt = {0: "80 MHz", 1: "160 MHz", 2: "160+80+80 MHz"}.get(width, "?")
    ldpc = bool(cap & (1 << 4))
    sgi80 = bool(cap & (1 << 5))
    su_bf = bool(cap & (1 << 11))
    print(f"  Channel width : {width_txt}")
    print(f"  LDPC          : {ldpc}")
    print(f"  Short GI 80   : {sgi80}")
    print(f"  SU beamformer : {su_bf}")

for pkt in rdpcap("capture.pcap"):
    elt = pkt.getlayer(Dot11Elt)
    while elt:
        if elt.ID == 191:                  # VHT Capabilities
            print("VHT Capabilities:")
            parse_vht_caps(bytes(elt.info))
            break
        elt = elt.payload.getlayer(Dot11Elt)

🧠 Knowledge Check
What are the two parts of the VHT Capabilities IE?

A 4-byte Capabilities Info field and an 8-byte Supported VHT-MCS/NSS set.

How many bits per spatial stream in the MCS map?

Two bits (values: MCS 0-7, 0-8, 0-9, or 'not supported').

The negotiated config is what of the two devices?

The intersection/minimum: narrowest width, fewest streams, lowest max MCS.
